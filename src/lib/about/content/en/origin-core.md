My story started in the 8th grade when I became curious about how websites actually worked behind the scenes.

At first I experimented with visual builders and generated HTML exports, but that quickly felt limiting. I wanted to understand what each tag and style did, so I switched to writing plain HTML and CSS by hand. That was the first important turning point: instead of assembling pages, I started learning web architecture.

Soon after, I explored simple PHP authentication examples with sessions and login logic. Even those tiny experiments taught me the relationship between frontend and backend state, and they gave me the first "this is what I want to do" feeling.

This core moment is why everything else exists: education choices, long-term project work, and eventually combining software engineering with astrophotography tooling.
