### **Education**

* **2025 – Present: Master's Degree & Thesis Development**
    I am currently pursuing my Master's degree in Computer Science at VŠB - TUO, specializing in Digital Image Processing and Computer Graphics. This program broadened my horizons with fascinating subjects and introduced me to **Rust**, a language I now heavily utilize in my own projects. 
    Currently, I am working on my semestral project and Master's thesis: an **Astro Grader software**. The application parses raw `.fits` images from astro-cameras. In the first phase, it uses conventional methods (FWHM, star count, background contrast) to filter out bad frames (e.g., star trails, clouds). For my final thesis, I will replace this statistical grading with a custom-trained neural network.
* **Sept 2024 – May 2025: Bachelor's Thesis**
    For my final year of undergraduate studies, I wrote my thesis on a *"Reactive frontend for an e-learning system."* I was responsible for upgrading the frontend of the university's Kelvin system from a static/semi-dynamic Svelte 3 setup to a fully reactive Vue3 architecture. This involved running both frameworks side-by-side during the transition, updating components, and expanding my backend knowledge by modifying Django API endpoints.
* **2022 – 2025: Bachelor's Degree**
    After high school, I enrolled at VŠB - TUO (Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science) majoring in Computer Science. My goal was to earn my degree and learn technologies I wouldn't naturally pick up on my own. I gained a deep understanding of memory management and data representation through C, learned the fundamentals of C++, C#, and Python, and mastered relational database design.
* **2018 – 2020: High School**
    I attended the Heyrovský Secondary Industrial School of Chemistry in Ostrava. I chose this path because I loved chemistry in middle school and preferred to learn programming self-taught at home. Ultimately, this self-driven approach allowed me to learn more about software development than a traditional high school IT curriculum would have.

### **Programming & Career**

* **June 2025 – Present: Software Engineer at Profiq**
    I joined Profiq for a three-month internship where I was introduced to **React** and the backend framework **NestJS**. I contributed to internal projects, including a new employee budget-tracking system, and learned valuable agile and team-based practices. After the internship, I stayed with the company and continue to help with smaller projects alongside my studies.
* **2022: Discovering SvelteKit & Full-Stack Development**
    My first encounter with a full-stack framework was Next.JS during a friend's hosting project (Skypad), but I found myself mostly copy-pasting React code without fully enjoying the syntax. Later, I discovered **SvelteKit**. Because it compiles code rather than using a virtual DOM, the syntax felt much cleaner and more intuitive. I adopted SvelteKit for all my web development going forward, starting with my personal site and a wiki for the GameRealms server. 
* **2020 – 2022: JavaScript, NodeJS & Discord Bots**
    During high school, I dove deep into JavaScript. I used jQuery and AJAX to build a seamless, reload-free information system for a Minecraft server. I also started working with NodeJS to create Discord bots, starting with a Self-Bot that tracked League of Legends statistics, which later evolved into a public bot named *Miňonka* (currently being rewritten into v3). To merge my interests, I built web tools in PHP and JS to simplify chemistry calculations, like a Q-Test calculator and a graph-to-equation solver.
* **2018 – 2020: The PHP Era & Early Projects**
    I began using PHP regularly, building everything from scratch without frameworks. I created an automated administration system for SinusBots that connected via SSH to deploy, configure, and manage bot instances. I also built a comprehensive ticketing, permission, and premium currency info-panel for the SurprisePlay Minecraft server. 
* **2016: How It All Started**
    My interest in web development began in the 8th grade. After experimenting with WYSIWYG Web Builders and messy HTML exports from MS Word, I decided to learn raw HTML and CSS. I soon integrated basic PHP session-based authentication scripts to understand how server-side logic and user logins worked.
